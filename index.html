MICRO-INVESTING FLOW

Simplifying Micro-Investing for Gen-Z

1. Project Overview & The Core Problem

The platform had high acquisition rates but a major drop-off occurred before users funded their accounts. Financial jargon and exposure to complex asset choices early on caused friction and cognitive fatigue.

Core UX Hypothesis: Users abandonment was due to psychological friction (anxiety of losing funds/lack of clarity) rather than aesthetic design.

2. Business Goals & UX Metrics

Target KPI Lift deposit onboarding completion from 61% to 72%.
UX Metric Reduce abandonment rate on funding selection panel.
Business Impact Lower onboarding-related helpdesk tickets by 20%.

3. User Research & Insights

Through unmoderated audits (Maze) and 10 moderated remote interviews, I synthesized the user anxiety cycle.

  • Users were confused by terms like "Diversified Portfolio Allocations".
  • A fear of making accidental deposits triggered hesitation.
  • Progressive disclosure was preferred over showing multiple complex panels on step one.

4. High-Fidelity UI & Design System

We introduced an empathetic interface using simple terms, progress indicators, clear error styling, and distinct touch targets.

  • Touch Targets: Interactive elements set to 48px height.
  • Readability: Color contrast ratio strictly aligned with WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1).
  • Microcopy Guidance: Clear statements near deposit buttons reminding users that deposits are flexible.

5. Usability Testing & Iterations

Early concepts had energetic gamified screens, but testing showed users found them unprofessional for finance. I reduced the saturated tones and moved security reassurance messages to the checkout card.

6. Final Impact & Deliverables

+18% Deposit Conversion
+12% 7-day Retention
-29% Time to Invest

ENTERPRISE SAAS

Optimizing Supply Chain Logistics Analytics

1. Project Overview & The Core Problem

The enterprise analytics interface was cluttered with numerous generic dashboards, resulting in long scanning intervals and failure to notice logistics exceptions (delays, errors) quickly.

Core UX Hypothesis: Organizers needed an exception-first structure, rather than a data dump, to allow operations managers to make decisions faster.

2. Business Goals & UX Metrics

Target KPI Reduce target transaction review time by 20%.
UX Metric Increase filter configuration and view-saving actions.
Business Impact Decrease related support questions regarding data queries.

3. User Research & Insights

Through contextual inquiries and workflow diagrams, I established key insights:

  • Users were spending too much time re-filtering data tables each morning.
  • Standard charts lacked visual contrast indicators for critical anomalies.
  • Siloed sub-panels caused users to keep multiple tabs open simultaneously.

4. High-Fidelity UI & Design System

I redesigned the system to emphasize exceptions. Sticky filters, custom layouts, saved views, and clear visual exception warnings were prioritized.

  • Exception colors: Saturated indicators with accessible ratios.
  • Typography hierarchy: Standardizing body weights to simplify scanning of dense data rows.
  • Saved Views: Inline quick-save utility above tables.

5. Usability Testing & Iterations

During our early testing, operators still felt overwhelmed. I removed three less-essential charts from the core dashboard layout and replaced them with a single anomaly notification banner.

6. Final Impact & Deliverables

-24% Review Time Cut
+31% Filter Adoption
-18% Support Tickets
